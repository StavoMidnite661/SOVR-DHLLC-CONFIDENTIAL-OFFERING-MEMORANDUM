BACKGROUND

The Disclosing Party is seeking a $12,000,000 senior secured credit facility and has made available a secure data room (the "Data Room") containing confidential information for review by the Receiving Party in connection with its evaluation of a potential lending relationship (the "Permitted Purpose").

The parties desire to protect the confidentiality of such information and therefore agree as follows:

AGREEMENT

1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether in writing, orally, electronically, or in any other form, including but not limited to: business plans, financial statements, projections, capital tables, offering memoranda, legal documents (pledge agreements, security agreements, UCC filings), technical documentation, product roadmaps, customer lists, contracts, and any analyses or summaries thereof. Confidential Information does not include information that is (a) already known to the Receiving Party without restriction, (b) publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party, or (c) independently developed without reference to the Disclosing Party's information.

2. Obligation of Confidentiality

The Receiving Party agrees:

  • a. To hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and to exercise a degree of care not less than that used to protect its own confidential information of like importance;
  • b. Not to disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
  • c. To limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, and professional advisors (collectively, "Representatives") who have a need to know for the Permitted Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement;
  • d. To immediately notify the Disclosing Party of any suspected unauthorized use or disclosure.

3. Permitted Purpose & Use Limitations

The Receiving Party may use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating a potential credit facility with SOVR Development Holdings, LLC (the "Permitted Purpose"). The Receiving Party may not use the Confidential Information for any other purpose, including but not limited to: investing in competing companies, soliciting customers or employees, or developing competing products or services.

4. Term & Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the date first written above and shall remain in effect for a period of three (3) years. Upon request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly (a) cease all use of the Confidential Information and (b) either return or destroy all Confidential Information (including all copies), providing written certification of destruction upon request. The obligation of confidentiality shall survive termination for any reason.

5. No License or Ownership Transfer

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any right, title, or interest in or to the Confidential Information or any intellectual property rights therein. All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party or its licensors.

6. No Commitment to Proceed

The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty that it will enter into any financing transaction with the Receiving Party. This Agreement does not create any binding obligation to provide financing or to accept any terms proposed by the Receiving Party.

7. Remedies

The Receiving Party acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement would cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party, for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. The Disclosing Party shall have the right to seek injunctive relief and any other equitable remedies in addition to any legal or monetary remedies.

8. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

9. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties concerning the subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements.
