C. Lender Review Workflow

Follow this sequential workflow to complete your due diligence efficiently:

  1. Initial Review (Day 1–2)
    • Read this Playbook (A–E)
    • Review Executive Summary & Use of Proceeds
    • Scan Document Status Matrix (B) to identify gaps
  2. Legal & Corporate Review (Day 3–4)
    • Read all legal documents: Certificate, Operating Agreement, Consents
    • Verify organizational authority and good standing
    • Check for any change-of-control or anti-assignment provisions
  3. Security & Collateral Review (Day 5–6)
    • Review Pledge & Security Agreement
    • Confirm UCC-1 filings (or pending filing status)
    • Verify control agreements for deposit and securities accounts
    • Assess collateral valuation (400,000 SOVR units pledged)
  4. Financial Review (Day 7–8)
    • Analyze historical financials (2022–2024) and YTD 2025
    • Review financial projections and use of proceeds budget
    • Confirm debt service coverage ratios
    • Check for off-balance sheet liabilities
  5. Business & IP Review (Day 9–10)
    • Review material contracts (customer, vendor, leases)
    • Examine IP Assignment documentation
    • Check open-source license compliance
    • Review product roadmap and technical architecture
  6. Compliance & Risk Review (Day 11–12)
    • Confirm all required licenses (Money Transmitter, etc.)
    • Review litigation and regulatory matters
    • Verify insurance coverage
    • Check for any environmental, IP, or employment risks
  7. Finalize & Sign (Day 13–15)
    • Prepare diligence exception report (if any)
    • Request final legal opinion and compliance certificates
    • Execute Credit Agreement and related documents
    • Fund the facility

F. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the due diligence process typically take?

A: We anticipate 10–15 business days from data room access to closing, assuming timely Lender reviews and no material issues arise.

Q: What is the security for the $12MM facility?

A: First-priority lien on all assets of SOVR Development Holdings, LLC, including 400,000 SOVR units (pledged as collateral). Additionally, a personal guarantee from the principal and a pledge of equity interests in the LLC.

Q: Are there any known material adverse changes?

A: No. The Company confirms that since December 31, 2024, there has been no event or condition that would reasonably be expected to have a material adverse effect on the business, operations, or financial condition of the Company.

Q: What licenses are required for operations?

A: SOVR holds an active Wyoming Money Transmitter License (License #[to be inserted]). We are also registered as a Money Services Business (MSB) with FinCEN (#[to be inserted]). No other jurisdictions have been identified as requiring registration at this time.

Q: How are third-party open-source components managed?

A: All open-source software used is under permissive licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD). The Company maintains an open-source inventory and complies with all attribution and copyleft requirements. See the IP Assignment Agreement and the Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) in the data room.

Q: What is the expected closing timeline?

A: Target closing is April 22, 2025 (15 days from Playbook issuance), assuming satisfactory due diligence and execution of all credit documents. Early close is possible if diligence proceeds smoothly.

Q: Who do I contact if I have questions about specific documents?

A: For document-specific questions, contact legal counsel (see Section E). For transaction or business questions, contact Gustavo Maldonado directly.
