F. Frequently Asked Questions

What's the total facility size and structure?

Answer: $12,000,000 senior secured term loan, with a $2,400,000 stand-by letter of credit (20% coverage). Interest at Prime + 2.5%. 36-month term. Draws in three tranches ($4M each) based on milestones.

What collateral secures the loan?

Answer: Primary collateral: 400,000 membership interests (28.57% of issued capital) pledged by the GM Family Trust. Security interest perfected via UCC-1 filing with Wyoming Secretary of State. Additional credit support: $2.4M stand-by LC.

When can the first drawdown occur?

Answer: Initial $4M draw is available at closing upon satisfaction of conditions precedent: (a) executed Credit Agreement, (b) UCC-1 filing receipt delivered, (c) Legal Opinions delivered, (d) Board Resolutions signed, (e) NDA in place, (f) Stand-by LC issued. Target: Day 7.

What are the key financial covenants?

Answer: (1) Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) ≥ 1.25x (quarterly); (2) Leverage Ratio (Debt/EBITDA) ≤ 3.0x (annual); (3) Minimum liquidity $500,000 unrestricted cash. Note: Current model shows breaches in 2025–2026; Company is revising assumptions or seeking covenant relief.

What's still pending that we need to see?

Answer: Critical closing items: (1) UCC-1 filing receipt, (2) Legal Opinions (Corporate + Security), (3) Executed Board Resolutions, (4) D&O Insurance certificate ($1M), (5) Stand-by Letter of Credit, (6) Final signatures on PSA (if not already), (7) Final Cap Table with actual capital account values, (8) Revised 3-Year Financial Model addressing covenant compliance.

Why does the financial model show negative DSCR in 2025?

Answer: The model is a template with placeholder assumptions shown in brackets [ ]. It has not yet been populated with actual pipeline data, customer LOIs, or validated revenue assumptions. The Company's finance team is building the final version with real numbers and expects to demonstrate DSCR ≥ 1.25x by Year 2 (2026) with reasonable assumptions. Alternatively, the Company may request a covenant step-up or 12-month holiday from the lender.

Is the data room secure and audited?

Answer: Yes. The data room (https://dc.sovr.world/) is password-protected and requires NDA execution for access. All document downloads are logged with timestamp, user identifier, and IP address. Access is restricted to qualified institutional lenders who have signed NDAs.

Can I share these documents with my internal team?

Answer: Yes, but only with your employees, officers, and professional advisors who need to review the materials for the permitted purpose (evaluation of the credit facility). All such persons must be bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as the NDA you signed. You are responsible for ensuring their compliance.

What happens after I complete my review?

Answer: Provide feedback to the Trustee (Gustavo O. Maldonado). Indicate any material concerns, requests for clarification, or additional information needed. The Trustee will work with counsel to address issues and move toward a term sheet or commitment letter. Once terms are agreed, the signing and closing process begins (target 5–7 days).

Who owns the intellectual property?

Answer: All IP (GitHub repos: SOVR-Oracle-Ledger, SOVR-Unified-Credit, SOVR-Credit-Bridge-POS, etc.) is owned exclusively by SOVR Development Holdings, LLC. IP Assignment Agreements are in place with all contractors, using the template provided in legal/IP-Assignment-Agreement.html. All source code is properly assigned and free of liens or third-party encumbrances.
