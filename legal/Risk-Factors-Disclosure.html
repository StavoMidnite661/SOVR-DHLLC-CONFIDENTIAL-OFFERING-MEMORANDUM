RISK FACTORS

AN INVESTMENT IN THE SECURITIES INVOLVES A HIGH DEGREE OF RISK. POTENTIAL INVESTORS SHOULD CAREFULLY CONSIDER THE FOLLOWING RISK FACTORS, AMONG OTHERS, BEFORE DECIDING WHETHER TO INVEST. THE COMPANY'S BUSINESS, FINANCIAL CONDITION, AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS COULD BE MATERIALLY AND ADVERSELY AFFECTED BY ANY OF THE FOLLOWING RISKS.

1. EARLY-STAGE BUSINESS RISK

The Company is in the early stages of developing and commercializing its technology platforms, including the SOVR Unified Credit system and associated products. There can be no assurance that the Company will achieve profitability or positive cash flow. The Company has a limited operating history, which makes it difficult to evaluate future prospects.

2. TECHNOLOGY & DEVELOPMENT RISKS

The Company's success depends heavily on its proprietary technology platforms. Significant delays or failures in product development, technical issues, security breaches, or inability to scale the technology could materially harm the business. The technology industry is characterized by rapid change, and the Company must continually innovate to remain competitive.

3. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE RISK

The Company operates in a heavily regulated environment, including financial services, data privacy (GDPR, CCPA), and consumer protection laws. Changes in regulations, enforcement actions, or failure to maintain required licenses could result in significant fines, operational restrictions, or loss of business. The Company's ability to obtain and maintain necessary regulatory approvals is critical to its growth strategy.

4. MARKET & COMPETITION RISK

The fintech and credit technology markets are intensely competitive, with numerous well-established players and new entrants. The Company faces competition from large financial institutions, technology companies, and startups. Competitive pressures could lead to price reductions, increased marketing expenditures, and loss of market share, any of which could adversely affect revenue and margins.

5. LIQUIDITY & CASH FLOW RISK

The Company has limited liquidity and may require additional financing to fund operations, product development, and growth initiatives. There is no assurance that additional funding will be available on favorable terms, or at all. The Company's ability to meet financial obligations, including debt service under the proposed credit facility, depends on achieving sufficient revenue and cash flow.

6. DEPENDENCE ON KEY PERSONNEL

The Company's success depends significantly on the continued service of its founding team and key technical personnel. Loss of any key executive or technical employee could disrupt operations, delay product development, and harm relationships with customers and partners. The Company does not have key person insurance on its executives.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RISK

The Company's intellectual property is critical to its business. There can be no assurance that the Company's IP rights will be adequately protected, will not be challenged, or will not be infringed by third parties. Infringement claims, whether with or without merit, could result in significant litigation costs, injunctions, or the need to license technology on unfavorable terms.

8. CYBERSECURITY & DATA PRIVACY RISK

As a financial technology company handling sensitive personal and financial data, the Company is a target for cyberattacks, data breaches, and identity theft. Despite security measures, a significant breach could result in substantial liability, regulatory penalties, loss of customer trust, and damage to reputation. Compliance with data privacy regulations requires ongoing investment in security controls and procedures.

9. THIRD-PARTY DEPENDENCIES

The Company relies on third-party vendors, cloud infrastructure providers (e.g., AWS, Azure), and technology partners for critical components of its platform. Service outages, price increases, or termination of these relationships could disrupt operations and harm customer experience. The Company's ability to maintain favorable vendor terms and ensure service continuity is important to its business.

10. DEBT Covenant & FINANCING RISK

The proposed $12 million senior secured credit facility will impose financial and operational covenants on the Company. Failure to meet these covenants could result in an event of default, acceleration of repayment obligations, and potential foreclosure on pledged collateral. The Company's ability to service debt depends on achieving projected cash flows, which are uncertain.

11. COLLATERAL VALUATION & ENFORCEMENT RISK

The loan is secured by a pledge of 400,000 membership interests in SOVR Development Holdings, LLC. In the event of default, enforcement of the security interest may involve complex legal proceedings and uncertainties regarding valuation of the collateral. There is no guarantee that the collateral value will be sufficient to satisfy the outstanding debt obligation.

12. LEGAL & LITIGATION RISK

The Company may be subject to legal proceedings, regulatory investigations, or claims arising from its operations. Litigation is inherently unpredictable and can be costly, regardless of outcome. Adverse judgments or settlements could result in significant monetary damages and harm the Company's business and reputation.
